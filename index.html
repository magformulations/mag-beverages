What Are Kavalactones — and Why Does the Source Matter?

Kavalactones are the active compounds in kava root (Piper methysticum) responsible for its calming, mood-lifting effects. They interact with GABA receptors in the brain — similar to how anti-anxiety compounds work — producing relaxation and social ease without intoxication. Not all kava is equal: noble kava chemotypes are smoother, safer for regular use, and free from the compounds found in low-grade tudei varieties. MAG's Spirited Kava Infuser bottle and Kava Shot combine premium noble kava extract with ashwagandha, L-theanine, and Vitamin C — a functional stack that stands apart from every sparkling kava tonic, kava spirit, and kava powder on the market today.
